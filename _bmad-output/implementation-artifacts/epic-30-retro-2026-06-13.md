## Challenges (systemic, no blame)

1. **S5 was significantly under-modeled by the story spec.** The spec assumed "73 pre-drifted files, A72 is the headline risk" and "the slice nets are the byte-identity oracle." Reality: ALL 74 files were prettier-CLEAN (→ `--write` is required, not banned), the nets do NOT assert the frame (→ byte-identity is by construction, not net-verified), and the frames were heterogeneous (`space-y-6` mains, non-`<main>` containers, non-portal siblings, bespoke headers). The A56 spike caught all of this at dev time, but it means the story's AC-2/AC-6 were literally contradictory to the correct implementation (→ P2 spec-correction).
2. **Parallel-agent divergence on a shared sub-decision (A101 recurrence).** Two finance subagents resolved the "space-y-6 main + canonical loading frame" case differently — one half-adopted (loading→PageShell, main inline), one left fully inline. The orchestrator's grep sweep caught the 5 half-adoptions and reverted them. Confirms A101: a parallel fan-out ALWAYS needs an orchestrator-level cross-slice consistency pass.
