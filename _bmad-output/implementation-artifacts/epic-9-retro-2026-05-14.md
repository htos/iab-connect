## 2. What went well

- **One review round, not four.** Epic 3 took four code-review rounds; Epic 9 took one. The A12–A14 mechanical dev-process checklists from the Epic-3 retro, combined with the mechanical nature of de-branding work, drove this down. The Epic-3 retro bet paid off. *(Harry's pick for biggest success.)*
- **Behavior-preserving discipline was consistent.** Every story explicitly reasoned about byte-identical output and NULL semantics; an unconfigured deployment renders unchanged. E9-S3 even marked its single intentional shipped-default change (`InvoiceSettings`) and justified it.
- **Sound escalation judgment.** E9-S3 recognized the Keycloak-realm-seed coupling and escalated (Q2) rather than half-fixing in isolation. E9-S4 documented a defensible, reasoned spec deviation rather than following the literal wording blindly.
- **A14 applied.** Every E9 story shipped its AC-named test files — zero "missing test file" findings in the boundary review.

## 3. What didn't go well — patterns

- **Un-owned recurring debt.** The pre-existing lint baseline failure (`members/segments/page.tsx`, `admin/backups/page.tsx` — `react-hooks/set-state-in-effect`) was flagged in **all four** E9 story completion notes and deferred **again** in the code review. It was covered by no Epic-3 A-item. Noting a defect repeatedly without ownership is avoidance, not deferral. → **A19**
- **Escalations without a home.** The Q2 Keycloak-coupling escalation lived only in a story completion note, resurfaced as code-review finding F11, and is still open. Escalations need a persistent location with an owner. → **A20**
- **Spec-caused bugs.** Two review findings were authored by the spec, not the dev:
  - **F20** — S2 AC-4 literally prescribed putting `settings.applicationName` in a `useCallback` dependency array; that exact instruction caused the edit-campaign edit-clobber race.
  - **F1** — S1 AC-6 placed `svg` in the upload allowlist without the AC considering the anonymous serving side → the Critical stored-XSS finding. New attack surface, only the write path threat-modelled. → **A21**
- **Review-patches without dedicated tests.** The 17 inline code-review patches passed the existing 1872-test suite (no regression) but new behavior — magic-byte sniff, NULL-preservation, the deps-race fix, field validation — has no targeted coverage. → **A22**
- **Incomplete de-branding grep token list.** E9-S4's final re-grep checked `iab-kulturverein` but not `iabconnect`, so `noreply@iabconnect.ch` slipped through and was only caught in the code review. → **A23**
