## Challenges (systemic, no blame)

1. **The story spec's "short residual tail" framing was inverted by reality (caught at Task 0).** The epic skeleton assumed a small tail; the live A56 import-scan (done at create-story) found 17 WRAPPED modules across 10 slices + the epic-gap (`email-templates.ts` = sole `ApiClient` consumer, required S1 scope to enable S2's `api-client.ts` deletion). The create-story refresh corrected this BEFORE dev ‚Äî so dev-story ran on an accurate map. Confirms A56/A62: the create-story import-scan is what prevents a mid-flight scope explosion.
2. **The post-deletion comment-sweep (A117) blurred two distinct gates.** "Zero references" naturally reads as one thing; it is two ‚Äî importer-zero (build-safety, the binding gate = import-grep + `next build`) vs string-reference-zero (cosmetic, ~119 provenance comments). The sweep was comment-only + tsc-re-verified, but the capture-based `sed` left ~5 tersely-degraded comments (`vi.mock("audit")`) ‚Äî a LOW cosmetic the boundary review flagged and we dismissed (basename stands in acceptably; a full rewrite across ~13 files is disproportionate). Lesson: gate on the import-grep + build, sweep comments as a separate cosmetic pass, and accept basename-substitution as good-enough.
3. **The flat-config ban had a clobber hazard (A116).** A naive repo-wide `no-restricted-imports` would have silently disabled the existing per-zone cross-zone bans (last-match-wins). Caught at design time ‚Üí spread-into-zones + catch-all-excludes-zones + a spot-verify that the ban fires in both a zone file and a catch-all file.
